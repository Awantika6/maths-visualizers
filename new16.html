01. Key Concepts

Gradient Descent (GD)

Stable & straight. Uses the 1st derivative of the entire data pool to move downhill.

The Logic: By calculating the slope of the entire mountain, we take a precise step toward the bottom. Highly predictable but computationally heavy.

Stochastic GD (SGD)

Noisy & jittery. Uses random samples to avoid getting trapped in local dips.

The Logic: We only look at one random point at a time. The "noise" allows us to hop over small, fake holes (Local Minima).

2nd-Order Methods

Fast & intelligent. Uses the 2nd derivative (curvature) to predict the bottom.

The Logic: By analyzing the curvature (Hessian matrix), we don't just know which way is down, we know exactly where the center is.
