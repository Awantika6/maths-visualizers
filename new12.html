Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors

The Eigenvector

Think of this as the "Steady Direction." When a matrix pushes, pulls, or rotates space, most arrows change direction. The Eigenvector is the rare arrow that stays on its original line—it never tilts!

The Eigenvalue

Think of this as the "Scaling Factor." It’s the specific number (\lambda) that tells you how much the Eigenvector stretched or shrank. If \lambda=2, the vector doubled in length.
