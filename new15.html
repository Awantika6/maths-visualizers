PCA

(Principal Component Analysis)

The Concept

PCA is the science of finding the best angle to view complex data. If data is a 3D swarm of bees, PCA finds the 2D "plane" where the swarm looks the widest. This direction of maximum spread is the Principal Component.
Task 01: Information Compression

The Flattening Stream

A 3D data stream is passing through a "Dimensional Gate." You must squash it into a 2D sheet. To keep the most info, do you squash it along the Widest axis or the Narrowest axis?

Note: You squash the Narrowest axis! By removing the direction with the least "spread" (Variance), the resulting 2D sheet keeps the maximum possible detail.
Task 02: Spatial Alignment

Finding the PC1 Axis

The green line represents your Principal Component. Rotate the data cloud until the green line perfectly tracks the longest path of the moving particles.

Note: The direction you just found is the Eigenvector of the Covariance Matrix. It points in the direction of the greatest variance.
Task 03: Relationship Sifting

The Redundancy Check

If "Height" and "Weight" in a dataset always move together, they are highly correlated. In PCA, do we keep both as separate components, or merge them into one axis?

Note: PCA specializes in Redundancy Reduction. If variables are correlated, they share the same Eigenvector. Merging them simplifies the data without losing the relationship.
